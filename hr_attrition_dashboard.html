Analyst Commentary
Key findings from exit data
100K Exits
  • Retirement is the #1 exit driver at 20.2% (20,161 employees), suggesting a significant ageing workforce challenge. Succession planning should be a top priority.
  • Career Change accounts for 20.0% of exits — nearly equal to retirement. This points to gaps in internal mobility and career development programs.
  • Evenly distributed departments (~16–17K exits each) indicate a company-wide systemic issue rather than a localized problem in specific business units.
  • Satisfaction scores are uniformly mid-range (avg 3.0/5) across all departing employees — no group reports high satisfaction at exit, confirming broad engagement issues.
  • Mid-career employees (36–45) form the largest exit cohort at 21,050. Losing experienced talent in their prime productivity years is a significant business risk.
  • Gender distribution is near-equal across all four categories (~25% each). Exit behavior appears gender-neutral, ruling out gender-specific retention problems.
HR Recommendations
🎯
Succession Planning
With retirement being the top exit reason, build formal succession programs targeting roles with longest tenure averages. Implement mentorship to transfer institutional knowledge before exits.
🚀
Internal Career Mobility
Career Change is the #2 exit driver. Create structured internal job boards, lateral move programs, and "explore a role" initiatives to retain ambitious employees who want growth.
💡
Engagement Uplift
Uniform 3.0/5 satisfaction across all exiting groups indicates systemic disengagement. Launch regular pulse surveys, manager training, and recognition programs to lift scores above 4.0.
